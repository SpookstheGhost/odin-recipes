Waffles

Description

Waffles are a type of batter-based food made by pouring batter onto a hot griddle or waffle iron with a pattern of small, deep pockets. The batter is then cooked until it is set and the pockets are filled with steam. Waffles are often served for breakfast or brunch, and can be topped with a variety of items, such as butter, syrup, fruit, whipped cream, and powdered sugar.

Waffles are believed to have originated in Belgium in the 17th century. They were originally made with a yeast-leavened batter, but today most waffles are made with a batter that is leavened with baking powder or baking soda. Waffles can be made with a variety of different ingredients, including flour, milk, eggs, sugar, butter, and vanilla extract.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps:

  1. Preheat a waffle iron according to manufacturer's instructions.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and whisk until just combined. Do not overmix.
  5. Ladle the batter onto the hot waffle iron and cook according to manufacturer's instructions.
  6. Serve immediately with your favorite toppings.
