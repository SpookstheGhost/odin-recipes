Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of pasta, meat sauce, and b√©chamel sauce, topped with cheese and baked until bubbly. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that is perfect for a crowd.

The origins of lasagna are disputed, but it is believed to have originated in Italy in the Middle Ages. The dish was originally made with sheets of pasta that were filled with meat, cheese, and vegetables, and then baked in a casserole dish. Over time, the recipe for lasagna evolved, and it eventually became the dish that we know today.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. In a large skillet, brown ground beef over medium heat. Drain off any excess fat.
  3. Add onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  5. In a medium bowl, combine Parmesan cheese, mozzarella cheese, ricotta cheese, egg, and parsley.
  6. Spread 1 cup of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  7. Top with 6 lasagna noodles, overlapping as needed.
  8. Spread with half of the ricotta cheese mixture.
  9. Top with another 1 cup of the meat sauce.
  10. Repeat layers.
  11. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  12. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  13. Let stand for 10 minutes before serving.
